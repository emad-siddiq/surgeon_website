Design system · v1.0 · April 2026

A quiet, warm system for a careful practice.

The visual north star for Dr. Ghulam Siddiq — a boutique bariatric and laparoscopic surgery practice. Cream paper, soft peach plaster, a single clay accent for action. Serif for voice, sans for work, motion that breathes rather than performs.

01  ·  Color

Cream paper, peach plaster, clay for the quiet action.

Every token here earns its place. Clay — a single restrained accent — carries all primary calls-to-action so it keeps its meaning. Lilac is a cool counter-surface for contrast, never a second CTA.

02  ·  Typography

Fraunces carries voice. Inter carries work.

Serif/sans contrast rationale: Fraunces’ warm opsz and SOFT axes give headlines a hand-set editorial feeling while Inter’s neutral rhythm keeps dense body copy effortless — they share a similar x-height, so weight transitions feel honest, not theatrical.

05  ·  Motion

It should feel like breathing.

Micro · 180ms

Hover, focus, tap

Buttons lift 1px, color eases to clay-dark, focus ring blooms to 3px. Easing cubic-bezier(.2,.7,.2,1).

Reveal · 500ms

Section entry

Text rises 12px and fades in, image mat fades from 0 to 1, staggered 80ms. One pass per scroll — no looping.

Ambient · 26–34s

3D aurora

The webGL field drifts like weather. Never syncs with the cursor. Respects prefers-reduced-motion: reduce → static gradient.

06  ·  Section-by-section

One thing per section, done well.

Hero

Left: display headline + two-line promise, one primary CTA, one secondary. Right: warm portrait with a floating “today’s openings” card. Aurora behind. Premium move: the aurora.

About

Two-column: a large editorial pull-quote in Fraunces beside a video thumbnail with custom play IconButton. Premium move: first-person voice in the pull-quote.

Consultation

Peach band. Conversation-first copy, form on paper card, trust microcopy beneath the submit. Premium move: the definition-list (When / Where / Fee / Insurance).

Distinctions

Horizontal row of lilac chips w/ years, pausing auto-marquee on hover. No logos rendered garishly — treat as quiet attribution. Premium move: unhurried pacing.

Services

Three-column cards, one warm / one cool / one warm. Image mat on top, tag + serif title + sans paragraph + ghost link. Premium move: alternating tone.

Bariatric procedures

Editorial two-up: big serif number (01, 02) beside plain-language explanation. No stock icons. Premium move: the numbers as type, not chips.

Transformations

Before/after drag slider with consent microcopy. Caption below in serif italic. Premium move: restraint — one story, not a grid of ten.

Location

Muted map tile with cream overlay + pin in clay. Address block as a definition-list. Premium move: the custom cream map palette.

Gallery

Asymmetric 3-row grid, peach and lilac mats alternating, click-to-lightbox. Premium move: the mat colors instead of white frames.

Footer

On cream, not ink. Four thin columns, generous leading, one clay-underlined email link, single serif sign-off.

07  ·  Open decisions

Five calls I’d like your ear on.

  1. 01

    Serif in eyebrow labels?

    My take: no — sans eyebrow keeps the serif moments special. Say if you want eyebrows to feel more editorial.

  2. 02

    Nav: HoverNavBar on cream vs. paper.

    I went cream/80 with blur so hero feels continuous. Alternative: paper bar with a 1px border. Slightly more corporate.

  3. 03

    CTA copy — “Book” or “Request”?

    “Request consultation” sets expectation that it’s reviewed, not instant. “Book” reads easier. I’d default to “Book”.

  4. 04

    Before/After: default open or closed?

    Default at 50%, static, with a subtle “drag” nudge on first scroll-into-view. Auto-animation feels gimmicky.

  5. 05

    3D aurora everywhere, or only Hero + Consult?

    Hero + Consult only. Elsewhere the aurora becomes wallpaper; scarcity keeps it precious.
